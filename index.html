LEGAL GAP · 07 / 18

◉ JURISDICTION_SCAN · FEED_07

The Law
Isn't Keeping Up.
🇮🇳 INDIA · DPDPA 2023

Contains loopholes permitting government data access without judicial oversight. Strong in name; weak in teeth.

🇪🇺 EU · GDPR 2018

Enforces strict consent, right to be forgotten, and heavy fines. Currently the strongest framework globally.

🌍 MOST OF THE WORLD

No comprehensive data protection law. The burden of protecting privacy falls entirely on individuals who aren't equipped to carry it.

> conclusion: in the absence of strong regulation, corporations write their own rules.
> conclusion: individual users cannot be expected to outmanoeuvre billion-dollar legal teams.

What You Lose

IDENTITY

Loss of control over who you are, how you're perceived, and what decisions are made about you — without you.

Personal Habits
Aren't Enough.

We also need to demand structural change. Both matter.

⚖️ ENFORCEMENT

Strong data protection laws with real consequences — not voluntary frameworks that corporations can simply ignore.

🔭 TRANSPARENCY

The right to know when automated systems make decisions about you — and the right to challenge them.

🗑️ DELETION

Real right to delete personal data — from platforms and brokers both. Not just "deactivate account." Actually gone.

🏛️ ACCOUNTABILITY

Stop treating personal data as something companies are entitled to take. It requires genuine, informed consent — every time.
